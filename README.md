## 🗂 Projects

### 🧩 Humane Ed Tech Design Pattern Rubric
**Hackathon · Building Humane Tech · San Francisco · 2024 · 🏆 1st Place**

A research-backed rubric for evaluating educational technology tools through the lens of humane and human-centered design. Built collaboratively with a team of five in under five hours, the rubric scores ed tech tools across five dimensions — cognitive design, emotional and motivational design, attention and focus, agency and autonomy, and social and ethical design — each grounded in peer-reviewed learning science. Includes a worked example evaluation of Khan Academy (4.3/5 — "Strongly Humane"). Aimed at students, parents, teachers, and ed tech developers who want to make more intentional choices about the tools they use and build.

### 🎙 The Sound of Power: NLP Analysis of UN Security Council Speeches
**School Project · UC Berkeley · 2025**

A computational linguistics study of how UN Security Council member states use language to argue their positions on sanctions, humanitarian aid, and ceasefires. Using a multi-method NLP framework — stance detection, rhetorical strategy analysis, topic modeling (LDA + BERTopic), framing analysis, and transformer embeddings — the project extracts over 450 linguistic features per speech across 2,069 meeting transcripts spanning 1950–2025. The key finding: linguistic variation in pre-vote speeches is driven more by resolution type than by country identity or geopolitical bloc, suggesting UNSC diplomatic discourse is highly institutionalized regardless of who is speaking.
